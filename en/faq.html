Gameplay Basics

Outbound features an auto-save system that creates a save point every 5 minutes of gameplay. You can also manually save at any time by opening the pause menu (Esc on PC, Options on console) and selecting "Save Game". There are 10 manual save slots available. The game also saves automatically when you sleep in your van, complete a major quest milestone, or exit to the main menu.

The main story of Outbound takes approximately 15 to 20 hours to complete. Players who want to achieve 100% completion, including all side quests, collectibles, and crafting milestones, can expect to spend 30 to 40 hours. A casual playthrough focused on exploration and building typically takes around 25 hours.

Sprinting at night was originally restricted in the launch version. However, starting with patch 1.0.8, players can now sprint at night after unlocking the "Night Runner" upgrade at the signal tower near the starting area. This quality-of-life change was one of the most requested features from the community.

Outbound's energy system revolves around three main power sources:

  • Solar panels — generate power during daytime hours.
  • Wind turbines — work continuously but output varies with weather conditions.
  • Battery storage — stores excess energy for nighttime use.

Your van's energy consumption depends on installed modules such as workstations, lights, and appliances. Balancing generation and consumption is a core gameplay mechanic. Read our full energy guide for detailed strategies.

Fishing is confirmed to be added in the upcoming 1.1 update. The feature was announced in the March 2026 developer roadmap and will include multiple fish species, a fishing rod crafting recipe, and fish-based cooking recipes. A teaser trailer showed players casting lines from the van's roof and from riverbanks. Check our fishing preview page for the latest details.

Yes, van customization is one of Outbound's core features. You can upgrade the interior with workstations, storage modules, beds, cooking stations, and decorative items. Exterior upgrades include solar panels, roof racks, additional battery slots, and paint jobs. The van serves as your mobile base, and its layout directly impacts gameplay efficiency. See our van upgrades guide for the full upgrade tree.

Platforms & Multiplayer

Yes, Outbound supports online co-op multiplayer for up to 4 players. You can invite friends through Steam, PlayStation Network, or Xbox Live depending on your platform. The host's world progress is saved, and all players share resources, storage, and quest progress within the session. Cross-platform multiplayer is supported between PC and console versions. Read our multiplayer guide for setup instructions.

Yes, Outbound is available on PC (Steam), PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, and Nintendo Switch. The game launched on all platforms simultaneously. All console versions support 60 FPS performance mode and 4K resolution on PS5 and Xbox Series X. See our system requirements page for full specs.

Collectibles & Resources

Redwood can be found in the Redwood Grove biome, located in the northwest region of the map past the river crossing. You need a Tier 2 axe or better to harvest redwood logs. Each redwood tree yields 3-5 logs and takes approximately 8 hits to fell. Redwood is used for high-tier van upgrades, reinforced storage modules, and premium furniture crafting. Visit our redwood item page for detailed locations.

USB Keys are collectible items scattered throughout the Outbound world. There are 15 USB Keys total, and collecting them unlocks lore entries, secret blueprints, and hidden achievements. Each USB Key contains an audio log or data file that reveals backstory about the world before the event that left you stranded. Finding all 15 unlocks the "Full Signal" achievement. Check our interactive collectibles map for all locations.

Community & Reviews

In early 2025, Outbound faced a review bombing incident on Steam after a group of players organized mass refund requests and negative reviews, claiming the game's launch state did not match pre-release marketing. The controversy centered on missing features and performance issues at launch. Square Glade Games responded with a public roadmap addressing the complaints. For a detailed breakdown, see our full analysis page.

As of May 2026, Outbound holds a 72% positive rating on Steam. The game is worth it if you enjoy cozy survival crafting games with a camper-van theme, cooperative multiplayer, and exploration-driven gameplay. Players who prefer action-heavy survival games may find the pace too relaxed. The developers have been consistent with post-launch updates, adding new content and quality-of-life improvements.
